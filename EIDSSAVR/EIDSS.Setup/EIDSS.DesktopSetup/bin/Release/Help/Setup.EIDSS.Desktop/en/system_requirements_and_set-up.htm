Overview

The Electronic Integrated Disease Surveillance System (EIDSS) strengthens and supports monitoring and prevention of dangerous diseases in line with “One Health” concept by integrating veterinary and human surveillance, passive and active approaches, case-based records including disease-specific clinical data based on standardized case definitions and aggregated data, laboratory data including sample tracking linked to each case and event with test results and epidemiological investigations. EIDSS has been developed under the Cooperative Biological Engagement Program (CBEP). It facilitates in collecting, notifying, sharing and analyzing surveillance data. It consists of several modules listed below together with a specification of tracked data.

Human Module

• Demographic data

• Disease-specific clinical data based on standard case definitions

• Epidemiological investigations

• Sample and laboratory tests tracking linked to a specific case

• Aggregate cases

Veterinary Module

• Avian and livestock cases

• Farm/Owner info

• Disease-specific clinical data based on standard case definitions

• Epidemiological investigations

• Sample and laboratory tests tracking linked to a specific case

• Penside tests

• Aggregate cases

• Active surveillance data

Vector Surveillance Module

• Vector surveillance sessions

• Specific individual vector forms depending on vector type

• Sample and laboratory tests tracking linked to a specific session

• Field tests

Laboratory

• Samples, tests assignment, tests results and batch tests

• Aliquots and derivatives

• Transfer in/out operations

• Links to clinical case data

Analysis

• Customized and predefined reports

• Statistic analysis

• Different ways of data representation: grids, charts, maps

 

Typically, a case is identified at a lower level site, and subsequently a physician reports the case using the case notification forms. That information is entered into the EIDSS application at the Sentinel Stations or at an Epidemiological Monitoring Station (EMS). Once the initial data is captured in EIDSS, it will notify an Epidemiology team to visit the patient and collect case investigation data, along with required laboratory samples based on the case classification. The investigation data is entered into EIDSS at the EMS and the samples are registered. The laboratory technicians receive samples and assign the laboratory tests based on the diagnosed disease. The laboratory test results will define the final classification of the case.

The EIDSS system will update the case information at all relevant sites levels throughout the case lifecycle. Extensive sophisticated analysis and reporting capability is integral part of the EIDSS application. A Geographical Information System is integrated into many of the reports to provide visualization of critical information.
