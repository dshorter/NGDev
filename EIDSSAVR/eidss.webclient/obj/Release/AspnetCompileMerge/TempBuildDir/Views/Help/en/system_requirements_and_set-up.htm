Typically, a case is identified at a lower level site, and subsequently a physician reports the case using the case notification forms. That information is entered into the EIDSS application at the Sentinel Stations or at an Epidemiological Monitoring Station (EMS). Once the initial data is captured in EIDSS, it will notify an Epidemiology team to visit the patient and collect case investigation data, along with required laboratory samples based on the case classification. The investigation data is entered into EIDSS at the EMS and the samples are registered. The laboratory technicians receive samples and assign the laboratory tests based on the diagnosed disease. The laboratory test results will define the final classification of the case.

The EIDSS system will update the case information at all relevant sitgs levels throughout the case lifecycle.
