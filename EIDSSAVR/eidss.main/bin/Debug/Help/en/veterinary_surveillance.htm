Veterinary Surveillance

There are 2 types of reporting in veterinary surveillance: passive surveillance and active surveillance. Surveillance is based on both passive and active data collection processes:

-When a clinician or a laboratory encounters a patient or sample indicating the presence of certain conditions or pathogens, there is a legal obligation to report the case to local public health officials. The result is a passive monitoring of the levels of the disease in the community.

-Active surveillance, on the other hand, is commonly referred to as "case finding." This occurs when the data necessary to monitor levels of a medical or social condition is sought out actively. This is accomplished through a variety of means, ranging from clinical record reviews to community surveys.

There are 2 ways to collect data in veterinary surveillance:

-Case-based: each record equals 1 or more diseases of animals on one farm (Livestock and Avian cases), case contains some basic information and reach back info including deep investigation of the case;

-Aggregate: aggregate case - each record equals a number of cases in the selected administrative unit and time period; aggregate actions - each record contains information about a number of actions performed in the selected administrative unit and time period.
